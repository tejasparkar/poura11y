Frequently Asked Questions

Accessibility refers to designing and developing digital content, products, and services in a way that allows individuals with disabilities to access and use them without barriers.

Accessibility ensures equal access and inclusion for all individuals, regardless of their disabilities. It allows everyone to fully engage with and benefit from digital experiences, promoting inclusivity and diversity.

Ongoing accessibility compliance can be ensured by regularly monitoring and testing your website or digital products for accessibility, staying updated with accessibility standards and guidelines, training your team on accessibility best practices, and engaging with accessibility experts or consultants for guidance and support.

Yes, accessibility can be added to an existing website. It involves conducting an accessibility audit, identifying and addressing accessibility issues, and implementing necessary changes and improvements to make the website accessible to individuals with disabilities.

In many countries, there are legal requirements for accessibility, such as the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) in the United States. It is important to consult with legal experts and ensure compliance with applicable accessibility laws and regulations in your jurisdiction.
